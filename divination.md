### Explanation of Bian Yao (變爻), Especially Seven (七) and Eight (八), and Others in English

**Bian Yao** (變爻, changing lines) are the dynamic elements in Yi hexagrams that determine divination outcomes and transformations. They are classified by numbers generated during consultation: seven (young yang, stable), eight (young yin, stable), six (old yin, mutable), and nine (old yang, mutable). In traditions like Gui Cang, seven and eight are central for unchanging, stable interpretations, while six and nine drive change in Zhou Yi. Details follow.

#### Background
Bian yao arise from the Yi's line structure: each hexagram has six yao, either yang (solid) or yin (broken). Divination methods produce numbers 6-9:
- **Seven (7)**: Young yang—stable, representing emerging vitality without change.
- **Eight (8)**: Young yin—stable, representing receptive potential without alteration.
- **Six (6)**: Old yin—unstable, changes to yang, symbolizing maturity leading to reversal.
- **Nine (9)**: Old yang—unstable, changes to yin, symbolizing peak leading to decline.

This numerology ties to yin-yang cosmology, where odd/even numbers balance forces. Gui Cang's focus on 7/8 reflects "concealment" (stable hidden truths), unlike Zhou Yi's "change."

#### History
From Shang oracle bones (c. 1200 BCE), numbers indicated omens. Zhou Yi formalized 6/9 for flux (c. 1000 BCE). Gui Cang emphasized 7/8 for endurance, as noted in Han texts (e.g., Zheng Xuan). Song reconstructions (e.g., Zhu Zhen) preserved this. Modern: 1993 Qin slips validated Gui Cang's 7/8 use.

#### Current Status
Primarily academic; Zhou Yi's 6/9 prevail in practice. Revivals in Gui Cang studies (e.g., 2025 publications) highlight 7/8 for modern mindfulness apps or Daoist rituals.

#### Supporting Evidence
- Textual: Zhou Li describes 7/8 in Gui Cang.
- Archaeological: Wangjiatai slips show stable omens via 7/8.
- Comparative: Mawangdui texts vary classifications, supporting historical diversity.

#### Objectives and Purpose
Objectives: Classify lines for omens—7/8 for persistence, 6/9 for adaptation. Purpose: Facilitate cosmic insight; in Gui Cang, promote stability and introspection for balanced living.
